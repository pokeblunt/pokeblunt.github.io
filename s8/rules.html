Rules

Draft rules

The draft pick order is reverse order of placement for last season. Players iteratively choose pokemon for their roster in a regular rotation (ex: 1 2 3 4 5 6 1 2 3 4 5 6 1 2 ...). A pokemon is defined by their national dex number. For example, if you pick pokemon No. 38 (Ninetails) then you get Kanto Ninetails and Alolan Ninetails. Pokemon from the same evolutionary line can belong to different players.

Battle rules

Use Smogon's Gen 9 Double OU rules with the following additions and exceptions.

  1. Roster clause. Players can only use Pokemon from their roster.
  2. Mega clause. The use of Mega stones and evolution is allowed.
  3. Previous generations clause. Pokemon from previous generations 1 - 8 are allowed. Also any moves learned by a pokemon in previous generations are allowed even if they cannot be learned in gen 9.
    • Pokemon from previous generations
    • Pokemon moves from previous generations
  4. Non legendary and non mythical clause. All non legendary and non mythical pokemon are allowed reguardless of base stats.
    • Slaking
    • Palafin
  5. Abilities clause. The following abilities are allowed.
    • Moody
    • Shadow Tag
  6. Evasion clause: Players can use the moves
    • Double Team
    • Minimize
  7. OHKO clause: Players can use the moves
    • Fissure
    • Guillotine
    • Horn Drill
    • Sheer Cold
  8. Gravity Sleep Clause: Players can use any move with Gravity
    • Gravity
  9. Legendary 600 base stat restriction. Legendary or Mythical Pokemon that start or can achieve above 600 base stats without mega evolving are not allowed.
    • Legendaries with > 600 base stats
  10. Abilities restriction. The following abilities are not allowed.
    • Power Construct
    • Wonder Guard

Tournament rules

Use the following rules for each tournament:

  1. Four players participate in each tournament.
  2. Each participant chooses a team of 6 pokemon to use for the entire tournament.
  3. Each participant plays all other players in the tournament exactly once.
  4. The player who won the most battles wins the tournament. If two or more players are tied with the most wins, all other players are eliminated and the remaining players repeat steps 2 and 3.

Season rules

Tournaments consist of 4 players and there are 15 tournaments this season (one for every combination of 4 players). This means each player will participate in 10 tournaments for a total of at least 30 matches.

All players advance to the playoffs. Players are ranked by tournament wins and then number of match wins if tied. If still tied, play single matches to determine an order.

Playoff rules

Players can use different pokemon for each match even within one playoff round. Seed a 6 person single elimination bracket (top 2 players get byes). The first round is best 2 out of 3. The semi-finals are best 3 out of 5. The finals and 3rd vs 4th place match are best 4 our of 7. The champion of the finals wins the season and picks their MVP (Most Valuable Pokemon) for the season.
